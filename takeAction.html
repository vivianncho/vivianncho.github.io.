Taking Action













Marine animals face many dangers due to climate change. Ocean acidification and higher temperatures kill algae which corals depend on. If the coral's die out reef fish can not survive as they depend on coral for food and shelter. Rising temperatures also kill many food sources, contribute to habitat loss, and change migration patterns. Stronger storms resulting from climate change threaten turtle's nests resluting in fewer offspring. Human activities and oil spills are causing marine animals such as the otter populations to become endangered. More information on the dangers can be found on the websites linked below the section "Informational website links to know more."







Ways you can help:


- To help marine animals to survive, we must transition away from our reliance on fossil fuels to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Carbon dioxide is a main factor to contributing to climate change.

- To make the ocean a safer place we can reuse, reduce, and recycle plastics, reduce pollution, and manage the fisheries.

Learn more specific ways to help at The Dodo website.
